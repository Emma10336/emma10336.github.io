A Verifiable Credential is a digital certificate whose authenticity any party can confirm independently, without contacting the issuer. The issuer's public key is published at /.well-known/did.json — anyone can fetch it to check the signature themselves. The forged version fails because modifying the content invalidates the original signature, and only the issuer holds the private key needed to produce a valid one.
